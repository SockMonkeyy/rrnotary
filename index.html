Acknowledgements

The purpose of an acknowledgment is for a signer, whose identity has been verified, to declare to a Notary that he/she has willingly signed a document.

Oath or Affirmation

An oath is a verbal promise to tell the truth made while holding the Bible. A witness may choose to swear an oath on another relevant religious text. An affirmation is a verbal, solemn and formal declaration, which is made in place of an oath.

Signature of Witness

A witness signature verifies the authenticity of a document and fulfills legal requirements.

Fingerprinting

Some employers require potential employees to get fingerprinted as part of their onboarding process. Fingerprints can be required for some formal/legal documentation as well. This is used as a security measure to protect against fraud and forgery.

A Notary Public is an official whom is appointed and authorized by state government, to serve the public as an impartial witness to deter fraud when performing acts related to the signing of important documents. Notaries are expected to follow the written rules set out by the state in which they are commissioned, to prevent such acts that may go against state regulations.

Notarization is the process whereby a Notary certifies a document. Usually this is done by attaching a notarial certificate to the document. The notarial certificate contains statements of fact or law in relation to the document being certified. Our Notaries often issue certificates in the language of the country where the document being certified is to be used.

A signing agent is a public notary who is expert in loan closing documents. A lender, title company, or escrow service may hire a signing agent to handle getting documents delivered, signed, or processed efficiently and on time.
